Home
​A soap holder is just a thing to keep your soap from getting all gross and melty when you're not using it. You might call it a soap dish or soap tray. It keeps the soap dry and clean, whether you're in the bathroom, kitchen, or laundry room.

🧩 Why Bother?

*   Keeps soap from sitting in water, so it lasts longer.
*   Keeps soap clean.
*   Keeps your sink or shower area looking nice and neat.
*   Can actually look good.

🧱 Types

1.  Wall-Mounted:

    *   Sticks to the wall near your sink or shower.
    *   Saves space on the counter.
    *   Usually uses screws, sticky pads, or suction cups to stay up.
2.  Countertop/Sinkside:

    *   Just sits on the flat place.
    *   Easy to move around and clean.
    *   Comes in all kinds of styles and stuff.
3.  Soap Lift:

    *   Simple kind with grooves that lift the soap up so water can drain.
    *   You can put it inside a bigger dish.
4.  Travel Soap Holder:

    *   It's got a lid, so you can take your soap with you.
    *   Great for trips or the gym.
5.  Magnetic:

    *   Uses a magnet to hold the soap in the air.
    *   Helps the soap dry out completely and stay clean.

⚙️ What They're Made Of

| Material        | Good Stuff                               | Not-So-Good Stuff                            |
| :-------------- | :--------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------- |
| Plastic         | Light, cheap, waterproof                 | Can look cheap, might get stained           |
| Stainless Steel | Lasts long, doesn't rust, looks modern   | Can be slippery, feels cold                  |
| Ceramic         | Looks nice, easy to clean                | Breaks if you drop it                        |
| Wood          | earth-friendly, looks warm             | Needs to stay dry, so it doesn't get moldy |
| Silicone        | Bendy, doesn't slip, easy to wash        | Can get soap scum buildup                   |
| Stone           | Fancy, heavy, lasts a long time         | Costly, needs to be cleaned regularly        |

💧 Ways to Drain Water

*   Slotted or Ridged – Water runs away from the soap.
*   Two-Tiered – Soap goes on top, drips collect on the bottom.
*   Suction Drain – Water goes right into the sink or shower drain.
*   Raised Grate – Soap stays above the water.

🧴 How to Clean It

*   Rinse it every week to get rid of soap junk.
*   Let it dry sometimes, so it doesn't get moldy.
*   Don't let water sit in the bottom.
*   If it's broken or moldy, get a new one (especially if it's wood).

🌿 Green Tips

*   Use soap holders made of stuff that breaks down easily, like bamboo.
*   Pick materials that can be used again.
*   Get one that holds two soaps.
*   Make sure air can get to it, so the soap lasts longer.

🧩 Things to Think About

*   Shape: Get one that fits your soap and sink.
*   Color: Make it match your bathroom.
*   How it sticks: Do you want it on the wall or sitting on the counter?
*   Easy to clean: Get one that's easy to take apart or simple.

🧠 Cool Facts

*   Soap holders started as fancy ceramic trays in old-timey bathrooms.
*   A good soap holder can make your soap last way longer by keeping it dry.
*   Some can even spread nice smells if you put them near heat.
